Executive Summary
This Software Requirements Specification (SRS) document defines the scope, objectives, and detailed requirements of ObservAI, an AI-powered analytics platform developed by Team 12 to help café and restaurant managers make data-driven decisions using real-time computer-vision insights. The document provides a complete description of the software system, including its functional and non-functional requirements, system interfaces, database design, and verification approach. ObservAI is designed as a web-based platform that integrates camera-based analytics, artificial intelligence, and interactive data visualization within a single management dashboard. Through live video streams, the system detects customers, estimates demographics, and visualizes spatial heatmaps to assist managers in understanding crowd behavior and optimizing their operations. The SRS also defines the characteristics of the sole user group—managers—highlighting the system’s focus on usability, accessibility, and ease of interpretation for non-technical users. Each major component of the system, including the frontend (React), backend (Node.js), and AI modules (YOLOv8 and OpenCV), is documented to demonstrate how they interact securely through HTTPS and comply with KVKK and GDPR regulations for anonymized data handling. In addition to defining the product scope, the SRS elaborates on software quality attributes such as reliability, maintainability, security, and logical database requirements. The preparation of this document involved close team collaboration, research on software-engineering standards, and iterative refinement based on instructor feedback. Throughout the process, the team faced several challenges, including balancing academic workload, ensuring consistency across sections, and adapting the report to formal IEEE SRS standards. These difficulties were addressed through regular team meetings, shared documentation practices, and constructive peer reviews, which collectively contributed to improving the clarity and coherence of the final document. As a result, this SRS serves as a structured and verifiable foundation for the future design and implementation of ObservAI. It ensures that all requirements are traceable, realistic, and measurable, providing a clear roadmap for building a reliable, scalable, and ethically compliant AI-based analytics solution for café and restaurant environments. The document establishes a robust baseline that will guide development activities in the upcoming phases of the project lifecycle. This SRS document provides a stable foundation for the upcoming design and implementation phases.

Product Scope

The software product under development is named ObservAI, a web-based artificial intelligence and computer vision platform designed for real-time customer analytics in cafés and restaurants. The system aims to provide managers with live visual insights into customer behavior, occupancy trends, and spatial usage through camera-based analysis supported by AI-driven interpretation.
ObservAI operates as a centralized, browser-accessible analytics environment that integrates computer vision capabilities with intelligent data visualization. It continuously processes live camera feeds to detect and count individuals, estimate basic demographics such as approximate age groups or gender distribution, and generate real-time heatmaps to indicate customer movement density and area utilization. These analytics are presented through a unified web interface, allowing managers to monitor and interpret on-site activity in an intuitive and data-informed way.
The application consists of a single administrative panel which serves as the primary point of access for authorized managerial users. This dashboard consolidates all analytics, AI insights, and visualization components. No additional user roles or public access levels are provided, as the application is designed exclusively for management use. The admin panel enables the viewing of live camera analyses, historical trends, and AI-generated suggestions regarding crowd flow, customer distribution, and operational optimization.
ObservAI does not include mobile or desktop native applications; however, it is fully responsive and compatible across devices such as tablets, laptops, and smartphones through standard web browsers. The design ensures that the platform maintains consistent usability and readability regardless of screen size.
From a technical standpoint, the platform’s front-end is developed using React, ensuring a dynamic and responsive interface, while the back end is powered by Node.js, managing data flow, model execution, and API endpoints. The AI modules—implemented using open-source computer vision frameworks—run inference on real-time video streams to produce continuous analytical output without manual intervention. A visualization layer transforms this raw data into interpretable formats, such as charts, demographic summaries, and spatial heatmaps.
The benefits of the ObservAI system are rooted in its ability to offer actionable intelligence directly from visual data without the need for manual data collection. It helps café and restaurant managers understand customer dynamics, optimize space usage, and predict peak activity periods through AI-assisted suggestions. In doing so, it provides a foundation for data-driven operational decision-making and improves awareness of real-time in-store patterns.
The product’s scope, consistent with the Initial Plan and Project Scope, deliberately excludes non-analytical features such as staff management, point-of-sale integration, and inventory tracking. ObservAI is not a complete business management system; instead, it focuses solely on transforming camera-based data into meaningful business intelligence.

User Characteristics

The ObservAI system is designed primarily for a single user group: café or restaurant managers. Since the software’s main purpose is to provide real-time analytics and AI-driven insights based on camera data, it does not target general employees, customers, or technical operators. Instead, its usability, interface, and access permissions are all shaped around managerial needs and capabilities.
 U1: Manager Users
Educational Level: Manager users typically possess at least a high school education, with many holding vocational or university-level degrees in fields such as business management, hospitality, or restaurant operations. They are not expected to have formal technical education in artificial intelligence, computer science, or software engineering.
Demographics: The primary users of the system are café and restaurant managers, generally between 25 and 55 years of age. They are professionals responsible for supervising staff, monitoring customer activity, and making operational decisions. Their work environments are usually small to medium-sized hospitality businesses.
Experience: Managers are experienced in handling day-to-day operations, evaluating customer behavior, and ensuring service quality. They are accustomed to using business tools such as scheduling dashboards, point-of-sale systems, and reporting applications. Their interaction with ObservAI is driven by the need to quickly interpret AI-based analytics and apply them to decision-making, rather than by technical curiosity or system administration.
Disabilities and Accessibility: ObservAI is primarily designed for indoor managerial use, focusing on comfort and readability rather than full accessibility compliance. The interface provides high color contrast, legible text, and adjustable visual components to support users with mild visual impairments. However, the system does not target public or multi-role accessibility standards, as it is intended exclusively for managerial users.
Technical Expertise: Most manager users have a basic to moderate level of digital literacy. They are comfortable using web browsers, emails, and analytics dashboards but may not have technical skills related to configuration, coding, or data systems. ObservAI’s design, therefore, emphasizes intuitive interaction, minimal setup requirements, and clear visual feedback, ensuring ease of use without technical training.
Devices and Environment: Managers are expected to access ObservAI from standard computing devices, including desktop computers, laptops, or tablets. The system is primarily used in office spaces or back-office areas within cafés or restaurants. ObservAI’s interface is fully responsive, maintaining consistent usability and visual clarity across different screen sizes and devices.


Product Perspective
ObservAI is designed as a standalone, full-stack web application that operates independently on its own server environment. The system integrates its front-end (React-based web interface) and back-end (Node.js + AI inference layer) within a unified architecture. While it functions autonomously, the backend exposes RESTful APIs, allowing potential future integrations with larger restaurant ecosystems or third-party dashboards.
For the current academic implementation, ObservAI remains self-contained, performing all core analytics, data processing, and AI computations internally. However, its modular structure ensures easy adaptability for broader enterprise environments, where camera analytics and AI modules could be integrated into existing management software through secure API endpoints.
Hosting for ObservAI will primarily depend on cloud services such as AWS, Render, or Vercel, ensuring scalability and remote access. However, due to budget and resource constraints, local hosting alternatives may be used during development or testing phases.

Hardware Interfaces
ObservAI connects primarily with IP cameras using RTSP protocols to fetch real-time video streams for real-time analytics. For testing purposes, this interface can also load pre-recorded videos. Each camera’s stream is processed by the backend AI system, which performs frame extraction and inference on GPU-enabled environments when available.
For simulations, the system supports local file inputs (MP4 format), ensuring flexibility during testing. The backend operates on standard x86-based servers or cloud instances with moderate GPU acceleration (e.g., NVIDIA T4 or GTX 1660).
No direct physical sensors or external hardware devices are used aside from cameras and the hosting server.

Memory Constraints
ObservAI requires sufficient memory resources to handle real-time video inference and analytics. The server should have a minimum of 8 GB RAM, with 16 GB RAM recommended, and if a GPU is used, at least 4 GB of VRAM is needed for stable processing of multiple camera streams. The system typically consumes around 1 GB of memory for two concurrent RTSP streams, while the local SQLite cache may use up to 1–2 GB of disk space. On the client side, modern browsers should have at least 512 MB of available memory, with 1 GB recommended to ensure smooth rendering of the dashboard and visualizations.

Logical Database Requirements

In a structured database environment, ObservAI keeps track of user credentials, camera settings, analytics information, alert logs, and insights produced by artificial intelligence. The system makes use of SQLite for short-term edge caching and PostgreSQL with the TimescaleDB extension for long-term time-series data. To guarantee consistency and effective data retrieval, relationships between users, sites, cameras, analytics records, alerts, and insights are established, and each table has well-defined attributes.
The database handles several categories of information:
User Accounts: Contain login credentials, email verification data, and access permissions for café and restaurant managers.
Camera and Site Configurations: Define café locations, associated cameras, and alert thresholds for each area.
Analytics and Heatmaps: Store real-time visitor counts, occupancy metrics, queue lengths, and spatial density maps generated by the AI engine.
Alerts and Notifications: Record unusual activity or crowding events detected by the system.
AI Insights: Contain natural-language summaries and recommendations produced by the integrated Gemini LLM model.
Different data types are accessed at varying frequencies.
Analytics and heatmap data are written frequently (every few seconds) by the AI engine, while configuration and user data are modified less often. Dashboard queries for visualizations occur periodically as managers interact with the interface.
Access control is managed through a role-based model.
Managers can view analytics, alerts, and insights for their own cafés, while system administrators have broader privileges for configuration and maintenance. Direct access to the database is limited to backend services and authorized developers.
Key entities include User, Site, Camera, AnalyticsRecord, Alert, and Insight.
Each analytics record references a valid camera and site to maintain data integrity. Constraints ensure that data such as visitor counts and queue lengths are non-negative, and time-stamped analytics remain unique for each camera. Alerts and insights are linked to corresponding analytics records for traceability.
Data integrity is maintained through primary and foreign keys, validation rules, and referential constraints. Deletion of a site automatically cascades to related cameras and analytics only when explicitly approved by an administrator. Audit logs record all major data modifications.
Data retention policies balance operational utility and privacy.
Short-term analytics and heatmap data are retained for up to seven days to support recent trend visualization. Aggregated summaries and AI insights are kept for up to three months for managerial analysis, as this period aligns with typical café and restaurant operational cycles and provides enough historical depth to identify meaningful behavioral trends without retaining unnecessary data. Keeping the retention window at three months also complies with KVKK/GDPR data-minimization principles and prevents outdated patterns from negatively affecting AI insight quality. User accounts and configuration data are preserved as long as the system remains active, unless deleted by an authorized administrator. All stored information complies with KVKK and GDPR data protection principles, ensuring anonymization, limited retention, and privacy by design.
Verification

This section outlines how the ObservAI software will be verified to ensure that it fully satisfies all of the functional and non-functional requirements mentioned in the previous sections. Verification activities include simulation-based validation, requirement-based testing, and a combination of automated and manual checks. These techniques ensure that ObservAI’s real-time analytics, visualization, alerting, and AI insight features operate reliably and safely in practical restaurant and café environments.
Verification Strategy
ObservAI follows an incremental verification approach aligned with agile development. To confirm that each implemented feature meets its corresponding functional and non-functional requirements, requirement-based testing will occur at the end of every development increment.
Verification will proceed in the following order:
Backend verification: API endpoints, analytics ingestion, rules-engine, session handling, and database operations.
Frontend verification: React dashboard components, real-time visualizations, interaction flows, and localization.
System-level verification: End-to-end testing confirming proper communication, synchronization, and data integrity across the entire pipeline.
Continuous Integration (CI): Automated build tests, linting, and backend unit tests on every commit to prevent regressions.
Most testing will be manual, supported by automated checks for key APIs, analytics pipeline functionality, and rules-engine threshold validation.

Discussions

In this part, we describe the issues encountered by our team in preparing its "ObservAI" project's System Requirements Specification document. In preparing the document, members undertook a series of analyses to achieve coordination in requirements. These problems encountered are classified into:
1. Limitations and Constraints
In preparing our SRS, some limitations are faced for our scholarly productivity:
Time Constraints: The preparation for the SRS was happening simultaneously with mid-term exams for various courses. It was difficult to manage both academic responsibilities and paperwork for projects. Time management helped to address both requirements.
Limited Documentation Experience: Although all members have a technical background, preparing a detailed and IEEE-compliant SRS document required understanding new standards and formal documentation methods, which initially slowed down progress.
Tool Limitations: The team relied primarily on freely available software tools such as Google Docs, Draw.io, and GitHub for collaborative editing and diagram creation. While effective, these tools had certain limitations in advanced formatting and UML diagram standardization.
2. Health and Safety Issues
No direct health or safety issues were encountered during the preparation of this document. However, extended screen time and late-night online meetings during the midterm period contributed to fatigue and reduced concentration in some team sessions.
3. Legal Issues
There are no legal issues related to the preparation for SRS. But since ObservAI uses camera analytics and image processing analysis, future compliance with data protection legislation like KVKK or GDPR will become important later.
4. Economic Issues and Constraints
As a student-led project without external funding, we operated under financial limitations:
No Dedicated Budget:  Lack of budget makes it harder to use commercial AI or UML design tools.
Resource Dependence: We exclusively relied on open-source resources that are freely available to use for our AI modeling. Although we conformed to sustainability principles in our project, there was a limitation in functionality.
5. Sustainability
During SRS development, sustainability was not emphasized. The major aim was to define requirements. In subsequent developments of ObservAI, sustainability will nonetheless be ensured since there will be optimization in computational resources used in camera analytics. 
6. Ethical Issues
There are no ethical considerations that came up in the entire documentation phase. The development team was aware of ethical issues related to using AI. These are related to anonymization of data processing in ObservAI. The solution does not involve face recognition to meet privacy-related ethical requirements.
7. Multidisciplinary Collaboration
All team members are students within the CTIS Department, sharing similar technical backgrounds. While this ensured alignment in terminology and communication, it limited interdisciplinary perspectives (e.g., design, psychology, or business management).
 Despite this, internal collaboration was strong, and roles were distributed efficiently among documentation, understanding AI model, and architectural design tasks.
8. Additional General Challenges
In addition to these specific limitations, there are some general issues that are encountered:
Communication and Coordination: Scheduling online team sessions was sometimes made challenging because team members' schedules conflicted due to courses or exam schedules. Online resources like Discord, Notion, Zoom and Whatsapp helped in asynchronous coordination.
Requirement Elicitation: ObservAI’s users (café managers) are hypothetical. Requirements are derived using assumptions made, prior research, or interviewing local café owners. There was a slight uncertainty involved in some user requirements concerning ObservAI’s user interface.
Technical Depth: Certain requirements like processing limits related to performance or AI inference speed limits demanded additional research to achieve realistic values. These values had to be verified to correctly represent computer vision processing requirements.
Formatting and Standardization: Aligning the document format with the official IEEE SRS template required additional review cycles, particularly for section numbering, heading consistency, and terminology alignment.
